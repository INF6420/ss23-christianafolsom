Adult Programing: True Crime Club

Join me as I share ideas from one of my favorite adult programs - True Crime Club. We meet monthly for an engaging, lighthearted discussion of this fascinating, yet disturbing genre. We focus our discussion around uplifting and empathizing with the victims of these crimes. Below are resources from some of my favorite cases.

UNSOLVED: The Murders of Biggie and Tupac

This case was a hard one for me! It’s a complicated story with so many different players and I ended up diving really deep to try and understand the worlds of gangsta rap, police corruption and 1990s LA that all converge together in this case. I still feel I have so much to learn! One of the most enjoyable parts of this research was becoming more acquainted with Tupac and Biggie’s music. Rarely do we get insight into the thoughts and feelings and art of murder victims. I was grateful to get to listen to the art these men created and to get to know them a bit better through it!


LAbyrinth by Randal Sullivan

In the book "Labyrinth," Randall Sullivan, an acclaimed journalist, delves into the controversial account of the murders of hip hop legends Tupac Shakur and the Notorious B.I.G., while exposing widespread corruption within the Los Angeles Police Department. Updated with new material, the book explores the unsolved murders, Detective Russell Poole's discoveries of officers connected to Death Row Records, and how this investigation sparked critical acclaim, lawsuits against the LAPD, and an FBI inquiry, making it the definitive story of these infamous unsolved hip hop murders.

Elizabeth Short: The Black Dahlia

I really enjoyed researching this case, even as I found it incredibly frustrating that it has yet to be solved! As we discussed this case in our group, we really went back and forth – finding holes and plausibility in each of the major theories. However, one big upside to diving into an unsolved case like this, is it really gave me an opportunity to focus on Elizabeth Short and her life. And as I learned more about Elizabeth, I found myself asking what SHE would think of the name “Black Dahlia” and how she would feel about the notoriety and press attention surrounding her case.


Black Dahlia Red Rose by Piu Eatwell

This gripping reexamination of the Black Dahlia murder presents new evidence and offers a definitive theory about this quintessential American crime that has remained an inscrutable cold case for over half a century. Piu Eatwell, a relentless legal sleuth, delves into the investigation, utilizing the recently unredacted FBI file, newly released sections of the LAPD file, and exclusive interviews with the suspect's family to present a gritty and cinematic retelling of this haunting tale, revealing the identity of the killer who brutally murdered aspiring starlet Elizabeth Short in 1947 Los Angeles.

The Turpin Family


The Family Next Door by John Glatt

Bestselling true crime author John Glatt tells the harrowing story of the Turpin family, who appeared to be a normal family but kept dark secrets that shocked the world. On January 14, 2018, a courageous seventeen-year-old girl escaped her Perris, California home, calling 911 to reveal the unimaginable abuse and torture she and her 12 siblings endured at the hands of their parents, Louise and David Turpin, in a tale of isolation, fear, and near-starvation that captured international attention. John Glatt provides a chilling and comprehensive account of this disturbing case, shedding light on the bravery of the siblings as they faced unspeakable horror.

Murdered and Missing Indigenous Women


Highway of Tears: A True Story of Racism, Indifference and the Pursuit of Justice for Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women and Girls by Jessica McDiarmid

Journalist Jessica McDiarmid delves into the heart-wrenching tragedies surrounding the 'Highway of Tears', an isolated stretch in northwestern British Columbia where Indigenous women have been going missing or found murdered for decades. Through intimate interviews with families and friends of the victims, McDiarmid sheds light on the devastating impact on communities and exposes the systemic racism and indifference that perpetuate the crisis, connecting these cases to a broader issue of undervaluing Indigenous lives in Canada. "Highway of Tears" is a powerful and essential account of the ongoing struggle for justice and the unwavering determination of families and communities to seek answers for the missing and murdered Indigenous women.

Child Stardom: Is it a Crime?


I'm Glad My Mom Died by Jennete McCurdy

In this candid and compelling memoir, Jennette McCurdy, known for her roles in iCarly and Sam & Cat, shares her heartbreaking and humorous journey as a former child actor. Facing eating disorders, addiction, and an overbearing mother's influence, she recalls her struggles and how she ultimately took control of her life, finding resilience, independence, and self-discovery. "I'm Glad My Mom Died" portrays Jennette's path to recovery and the realization of her true desires with unflinching detail and dark humor, offering an inspiring tale of personal growth and the simple joy of taking charge of her own life.

The Donner Party


The Indifferent Stars Above by Daniele James Brown

In April 1846, twenty-one-year-old Sarah Graves embarked on a perilous journey with her family and husband, joining a group of pioneers led by George Donner, heading west to California. As winter closed in, they found themselves trapped in the Sierra Nevada Mountains, and Sarah, along with fourteen others, embarked on a treacherous snowshoe trek to California, enduring unimaginable hardships and horrors. In this gripping narrative, bestselling author Daniel James Brown sheds new light on the legendary Donner Party's story, offering a spellbinding and informative tale that follows every agonizing step of Sarah's harrowing journey.
